<p><ul><li>Chemical Vapor Deposition</li><li>Liquid Impregnation Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The C-C composite material market includes various production methods, notably Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process (LIP). CVD involves depositing carbon onto a substrate, creating a dense and uniform composite with exceptional mechanical and thermal properties. In contrast, LIP entails impregnating a fibrous carbon substrate with a liquid precursor that solidifies, enhancing toughness and structural integrity. Both methods facilitate the customization of properties like strength and thermal resistance, driving demand across aerospace, defense, and automotive sectors.</p></p>
