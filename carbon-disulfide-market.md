<p><p>The carbon disulfide market can be segmented based on production methods: the natural gas method and the coke method. The natural gas method utilizes methane as a raw material to produce carbon disulfide, offering a cleaner process with lower emissions. In contrast, the coke method derives carbon disulfide from carbon-rich materials like coal or petroleum coke, resulting in higher emissions but potentially lower production costs. Both methods cater to varied industrial applications, impacting pricing and market dynamics.</p></p>
