<p><p>The Conductive Silicone market includes elastomers, resins, and gels, each serving distinct applications. Elastomers are flexible, durable materials used in seals, gaskets, and EMI shielding due to their resilience and conductivity. Resins are rigid, offering strong adhesion and thermal stability, ideal for coatings and encapsulations in electronics. Gels are soft, pliable substances used for thermal management and cushioning in sensitive devices. All types provide electrical conductivity, thermal resistance, and versatility, catering to industries like automotive, aerospace, and electronics.</p></p>
