<p><p>The halogen-free fire retardant additives market primarily includes magnesium hydroxide and aluminum trihydrate flame retardant additives. Magnesium hydroxide acts by releasing water vapor when heated, effectively cooling the material and inhibiting combustion. Aluminum trihydrate, on the other hand, decomposes at elevated temperatures to release water and form aluminum oxide, providing a barrier against flames. Both additives are environmentally friendly alternatives to traditional halogenated compounds, making them popular in various applications, including construction, electronics, and textiles.</p></p>
