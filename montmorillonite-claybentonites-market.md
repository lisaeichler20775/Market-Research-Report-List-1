<p><p>Montmorillonite clay, commonly known as bentonite, is categorized into three primary types based on its dominant cation: sodium, calcium, and potassium. Sodium bentonite is highly swellable and primarily used in drilling fluids and sealants. Calcium bentonite is less expansive, making it suitable for various industrial applications and as a binder in foundries. Potassium bentonite is favored for agricultural uses due to its nutrient content. Each type serves distinct markets, driven by their unique properties and applications across various industries.</p></p>
